# Market notes: AI script-coverage tools (2026-07-10)

Source: Scriptation's roundup of AI coverage tools (Screenplay IQ, Prescene,
Slated, Callaia, RivetAI, Scriptreader.AI, Premium Screenplay, Vondy,
Greenlight Coverage), read against ScreenScore as it stands.

## The headline

**Every tool on the list is a cloud service.** Not one runs locally, and none
advertises verified citations or honest cross-draft comparison. ScreenScore's
three moats — script never leaves the machine, every score carries verified
in-script evidence, revisions scored blind with regressions reported — are
each unoccupied in this field. Position accordingly; don't chase parity.

## Worth adopting (cheap, aligned)

1. **WGA-compliance language** (Screenplay IQ leads with it). Writers' real
   fear is their unreleased script becoming training data. ScreenScore's
   architecture *is* the answer — say it in those words on the landing page:
   local models, nothing trains on your script, nothing is retained anywhere.
2. **"Ask the script" is market-validated** (Prescene's Q&A is its flagship;
   an agency claims 95% coverage-time reduction). Our fast-follow should move
   up the queue — the stored digest + report make it mostly plumbing.
3. **Production breakdown exports** (RivetAI's core). We already parse
   locations, INT/EXT, night exteriors, cast-per-scene — a breakdown
   table/CSV export is nearly free and opens the producer/AD audience.
4. **Trust demos** (Callaia publishes sample coverages of Award-winning
   films). Publish the full Big Fish report (freely distributed script) as a
   living sample linked from the landing page.
5. **Price anchor:** cloud coverage runs ~$65/script. Free + local is a
   headline, not a footnote.

## Positioning notes (defend, don't copy)

- Screenplay IQ *rejects scoring* as a feature ("no scores, constructive
  only"). Our counter is not more scores — it's **scores with receipts**:
  a categorical score whose evidence you can click is a different product
  than a number from a black box.
- Slated/Prescene sell industry access and market data — cloud-native
  advantages we cannot and should not chase; they'd break the privacy
  promise (external lookups).
- Hybrid human+AI (Premium Screenplay) is a service business, not a tool
  feature. Out of scope.

## Candidate roadmap effects

- Promote: "Ask the script" (fast-follow → next), production breakdown
  export (new, small), landing-page copy: WGA framing + sample report +
  price contrast (small).
- Unchanged: no cloud mode, no external market-data lookups, comps stay
  labeled model-opinion.
